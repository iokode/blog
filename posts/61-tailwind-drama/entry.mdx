Recently, Tailwind Labs, the company behind Tailwind CSS, announced that they laid off 75% (three persons) of its
engineering team due to financial problems.

## A summary of what happened

I'm publishing this post some days ago of the news, so probably you already know what happened. This is not an
actuality blog (¿blog de actualidad en inglés?), so I don't care about arriving late.

In case you lived in a cave the last week, you can read the history in [this post](https://dev.to/kniraj/tailwind-css-lays-off-75-of-engineering-team-as-ai-tools-disrupt-revenue-model-1l3d),
but here is a simple summary.

Tailwind is an open-source library under the [MIT license](https://opensource.org/license/mit). Their business model consists of selling related paid
products using the documentation as conversion funnel. The first time I saw something similar model was in the Laravel
PHP framework, the framework is free, but there are some paid products and services associated with Laravel that
are referenced in the documentation.

Due to the LLM disruption, a lot of developers directly ask to LLM instead of navigating the documentation, so they stop
converting thought the documentation, and that leaded to a financial crisis that resulted in three of the four engineers
laid off.

## The rejected Pull Request

In the tailwindcss.com GitHub repository, [a PR that generates a llms.txt file were submitted](https://github.com/tailwindlabs/tailwindcss.com/pull/2388).
After some days of inactivity, an user asked why is not moving and Adam, the creator of Tailwind CSS replied:

> Have more important things to do like figure out how to make enough money for the business to be sustainable right now. And making it easier for LLMs to read our docs just means less traffic to our docs which means less people learning about our paid products and the business being even less sustainable.
>
> Just don't have time to work on things that don't help us pay the bills right now, sorry. We may add this one day but closing for now.

## The relationship with OpinionatedFramework and IOKode

OpinionatedFramework is nowadays a project in very early without documentation and specific webpage, but I
fantasized that someday could be an important project in the dotnet ecosystem. In that case, using Laravel
as a reference, I planned to offer similar products like paid packages and funnel them from the blog
(I think documentation is not a good place for advertising). The Tailwind case moved me to reflexionate about
that business idea because due to the same reason the Tailwind documentation lost traffic, the blog will lose it too.

In my case, I have a very strong opinion about LLM: they are here and will always be with us, and we need to adapt
to them. That is the reality, regardless if we like or not. As Ayn Rand said, *we can ignore the reality but not the
consequences of ignoring the reality*. In this case, ignoring LLMs means to be impossible to compete and be relegated to
forget.

Due that, [I decided that I authorize to use everything I wrote (code, docs, tweets...) to train LLMs](https://x.com/Montyclt/status/1759904379400810596)
and, of course, to be used by LLMs as provided context. Following this philosophy, a PR like the Tailwind's PR probably
would be accepted in the IOKode projects. If I don't accept it, it would be due to other reasons
(vendor-locking, bad practices, etc.) but not to avoiding LLMs to access the data with the intention to move the users
to the documentation.
